> **Qi Zhong** is the [[Tian Xia]] [[Deity|deity]] of [[Magic|magic]] and [[Medicine|healing]]. Originally worshiped by the [[Tian-Shu]], he is now popular in [[Jinin]], [[Quain]], [[Tianjing]], [[Xidao]], and [[Zi Ha]]. His [[Holy symbol|holy symbol]] is a wagon wheel with spokes made of the five [[Element|elements]]: water, earth, metal, fire, and wood. Qi Zhong is depicted as a kindly looking older man who is dressed in healer's robes that shift between the five elements.


## History

> Qi Zhong is closely tied to the pantheon of Tian Xia, at [[Shizuru|Shizurus]] request, it was Qi Zhong who resurrected the [[Moon|moon]] god [[Tsukiyo]] after he was murdered by his brother [[Fumeiyoshi]]. It was Qi Zhong who originally introduced an understanding of the five elements to [[Mortal|mortals]] and instructed them in their relationship with [[Magic|magic]], [[Medicine|medicine]], and martial arts.


## Church of Qi Zhong

> Qi Zhong is primarily worshipped by sages, scholars, and [[Healer|healers]] including those who practice traditional Tian medicine. Qi Zhong is also worshipped by [[Wizard|wizards]] and, in particular, many of his worshippers are [[Mystic theurge|mystic theurges]] who blend [[Arcane|arcane]] and [[Divine magic|divine magic]]. Qi Zhong's faithful believe it is their duty to share their knowledge and healing abilities with others.
> One sect of Qi Zhong worshipers has combined his [[Divine magic|divine magic]] with [[Alchemy|alchemy]], though this is not a widely accepted form of his worship. Their practices can be found in the five-volume [[Scroll|scroll]] known as the *Five Spoked Cycle*. Qi Zhong is said to have taught the secrets of [[Pei Zin]] [[Herbalism|herbalism]] with [[Irori]] prior to the Master of Masters' [[Ascension|ascension]].
